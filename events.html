Programming Competition

Fall 2015 Contest Results and Solutions
It's time for the ACM's Spring Programming Contest! This contest is open to all K-State students of all majors, so gather your friends and get your team signed up ASAP! Free food and drinks will be provided before, during, and after then competition. We also have over $3,000 in prizes! Teams may register on the day of the competition, but registration space is limited and is awarded on a first come, first served basis. To guarantee your team a spot, complete and submit this form as soon as possible. If you need to make a change to your registration, you can email ksuacm@ksu.edu to make adjustments.


When: TBA
Where: CIS Computer Labs
What: With up to two (2) friends, compete as a team to solve programming problems!
Why: Challenge yourself and have fun programming, enjoy free food and drinks, and get a chance to win!


Prizes Include:

  • 1st Place: TBA
  • 2nd Place: TBA
  • 3rd Place: TBA
One prize per team member. Prizes will be awarded shortly after the contest so make sure to stick around! 


Programming Contest Schedule:
9:30 AM: Team Check-In and Walk-In Registrations
11:00 AM: Contest Begins!

4:15 PM: Sponsor Announcements and Prizes (Winners should stay for Photos)


Rules and Information:

  • Teams may consist of up to three (3) students. Teams may only have one (1) graduate student.
  • Teams may bring as many printed materials (including textbooks) as they would like to use as reference material during the contest. Electronic devices and use of online resources is generally not permitted during the contest.
  • Programming languages you may use include: C, C++, Java, or Python.

PC LAN Parties

A LAN party is a gathering of cool people like you that love to play video games against each other. We usually set up around 7:00 and play until we can’t see the screens from fatigue, which is usually around sunrise. There are no official tournaments or games so feel free to bring your favorite!

What to Bring
Some things you want to bring include: yourself, your computer and games, a headset/headphones, and your friends! Please leave your speakers at home.

Console LAN Parties

A LAN party is a gathering of cool people like you that love to play video games against each other. We usually set up around 7:00 and play until we can’t see the screens from fatigue, which is usually around sunrise. Console games that are usually available include: Super Smash Bros, Mario Kart, Mario Party, Halo, Rock Band, Guitar Hero, and Street Fighter. There will be a Smash Tournament as well as a Guitar Hero tournament. Feel free to bring your own games and controller(s)!

Smash Tournament
We will be hosting a friendly Smash Tournament using the Project M 3.5 soft mod for Brawl. The signups will begin at 7:00 p.m. and run until 7:45 p.m. The tournament will begin at 8:00 p.m. The rules will be as follows:

  • 4 Stocks
  • 8 Minutes
  • No Items
  • All Characters Available
If there is a request to switch characters, the winner will switch first if they wish to 1221 Picking Banned stages will be struck out on the stage select screen There may be some confusion about how to do picking and counter picking. The rules will be explained in depth during the 15 minutes between signups closing and the tournament starting. Check out Smash Lab on Twitch!


Guitar Hero Tournament
We will also be hosting a friendly Guitar Hero Tournament. The rules for the tournament are:

  • Two competitors play a best of three rock paper sissors match to determine who will choose the first song. Song picking for the rest of the match alternates players.
  • Both competitors play a Pro Face Off on Expert difficulty.
  • The difficulty can be lowered to a level that is agreed upon by both players.
  • Each match is the best of three.

Hour of Code

The 'Hour of Code' is a nationwide initiative by Computer Science Education Week and Code.org to introduce millions of students to computer science and computer programming. You can find more information online at http://hourofcode.com. This event is open to youth of all ages! During the event, K-State faculty and students will lead youth in hour-long age-appropriate Computing Science and Programming activities based in a visual programming language called Scratch.
